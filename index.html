Bhadla Solar Project

Phases
Different phases of the project
01
Phase 1

Phase-I has seven solar plants with a total of 75 MW capacity. The first phase of the construction, which started in July 2015, was put into service in 2018. Phase one was created by the RSDCL.

02
Phase 2

Phase-II has ten solar plants of a total 680 MW capacity. In April 2019, the second phase was completed. Phase two was created by the RSDCL.

03 & 04
Phase 3 & 4

Phase - III consists of 10 solar power plants, each with total capacities of 1000MW and 500MW. In March 2020, the third and fourth phases began. The third phase was constructed by Saurya Urja Company. The Adani Renewable Energy Park designed the project's last phase.

Positives
Various positives of the Bhadla park

The Bhadla Solar Power Plant makes a significant contribution to the environment as well as to the nation. Let's go over some of the park's many advantages:
- The Bhadla Solar Power Plant project is significantly reducing India's reliance on fossil fuels.
- Numerous jobs for residents of other states have also been created by the influx of workers into the renewable energy sector.

- It is one of the key elements promoting regional economic expansion. The effect extends beyond the local region and has an impact on the entire nation's economy.

- The construction of the Bhadla Solar Power Plant in Jodhpur has improved the city's air quality because less fossil fuel is now burned for energy.

- Pure solar energy is used to produce electricity at the Bhadla Solar Park. It serves as a reliable source of green energy supply to the national grid. Up to 732,874 MWh can be produced annually by the Bhadla Solar Plant.
